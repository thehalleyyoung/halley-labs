# Review: Causal Identification and Posterior-Predictive Shields for Provably Safe Adaptive Trading

**Reviewer:** Yuan Cheng (Probabilistic Modeling & Stochastic Processes)
**Score: 8/10**

---

This project's probabilistic architecture is impressively rigorous, particularly in its treatment of regime inference and the Bayesian-to-frequentist bridge via the Posterior Shield Soundness Theorem. The Sticky HDP-HMM (Fox et al., 2011) for regime inference is the correct choice: the Dirichlet process prior allows the number of regimes to be inferred from data rather than fixed a priori, and the sticky parameter discourages rapid switching—both essential for financial time series where regime persistence is a stylized fact. The practical cap at K <= 5 regimes is well-justified both empirically (financial literature consistently identifies 3-5 macro regimes) and computationally (O(K^2) scaling in shield verification). The beam sampling implementation running in O(TK^2) per MCMC iteration with T = 5,000 daily observations and convergence in ~500 iterations (~50 seconds) is computationally tractable and realistic. The use of Student-t emissions (BIC 5552 vs. 6131 for Gaussian) demonstrates appropriate attention to heavy-tailed financial returns, though the choice of Student-t versus other heavy-tailed distributions (stable, generalized hyperbolic) deserves further justification through model comparison metrics beyond BIC.

The Posterior Shield Soundness Theorem is the project's crown jewel from a probabilistic perspective. The PAC-Bayes bound—converting Bayesian beliefs into frequentist safety certificates where the bound tightens as posterior concentration increases through the KL term—elegantly solves the known-MDP limitation of Alshiekh et al.'s shields. The use of a Catoni-style bound rather than the standard McAllester bound is technically correct for 0-1 specification satisfaction loss and shows sophistication in the PAC-Bayes literature. However, the sequential extension (holding uniformly over time as the posterior updates) requires a sequential PAC-Bayes bound in the spirit of Jun and Orabona (2019), and adapting this to the temporal-logic 0-1 loss is non-trivial—the problem statement correctly identifies this as Hard Subproblem 4 (~12K LoC with 6K Lean 4 proofs). The convergence analysis for the coupled regime-causal inference (EM-style alternation) is the weakest probabilistic component: the chicken-and-egg problem between regime labels and causal structure is acknowledged, but convergence to a fixed point that is identifiable under the stated assumptions (faithfulness, minimum-regime-duration, ANM noise-independence) requires a formal proof that the EM landscape has no spurious fixed points, which is challenging for the nonparametric ANM setting. The 4-stage error decomposition in the implementation (regime, causal, shield, composition) with independently verifiable bounds at each stage is excellent probabilistic engineering, providing auditability at every level of the inference hierarchy.

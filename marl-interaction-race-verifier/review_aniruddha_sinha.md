# Review: MARACE — Interaction Race Detection via Happens-Before–Aware Abstract Interpretation for Multi-Agent RL Safety Verification

**Reviewer:** Aniruddha Sinha (Model Checking and AI Applicant)
**Score: 7/10**

---

MARACE's core contribution — the HB-aware abstract interpretation framework over zonotope domains — represents a genuinely novel synthesis of concurrency verification and neural network abstract interpretation. The key insight of restricting the abstract domain's concretization function to HB-consistent joint states, rather than arbitrary Cartesian products, is both theoretically sound and practically necessary: the reduction from exponential (O(|S|^N)) to compositional (O(Σ|S|^{k_i})) cost is the difference between intractability and laptop feasibility. The convergence proof for ReLU networks — bounding the ascending chain length at O(D·W·|schedule_windows|) — is a meaningful result that provides predictable termination guarantees, and the fallback to sound per-iteration over-approximation for non-ReLU architectures is the right engineering compromise. The compositional assume-guarantee decomposition via interaction groups derived from HB graph connected components is algorithmically clean: the soundness proof (any global race trace projects onto at least one interaction group) follows naturally from the causal closure property, and the adaptive group merging when new interaction patterns emerge during analysis shows attention to practical robustness. The 12-stage pipeline architecture, with its progression from trace construction through HB graph building, group decomposition, abstract interpretation, adversarial search, and importance sampling, implements a well-structured verification workflow at 47,445 LoC.

The state-space management concerns are significant, however. The zonotope generator reduction via Girard's PCA-based merging to 2d generators introduces approximation error that is not bounded in the paper — for the 10-dimensional joint state spaces in the highway scenarios, this means at most 20 generators, which may be insufficient to capture the correlations between agents' states introduced by environment-mediated causal chains. The widening operator's convergence guarantee depends on the HB-consistency constraints being a fixed conjunction of linear inequalities, but the adaptive group merging changes this constraint set during analysis; the paper does not address whether re-running the fixpoint computation after a merge invalidates the partial convergence achieved pre-merge, or whether there is a monotonicity argument ensuring that merging only strengthens the abstract element. The MCTS adversarial search over the schedule space uses abstract safety margins as UCB1 value estimates, but the relationship between the abstract margin (over-approximate) and the concrete distance to violation is not formally characterized — over-approximation guarantees soundness of the search but may degrade the search efficiency substantially if the abstraction is too coarse. The 91% state coverage at 12.3 seconds for 4-agent highway is impressive, but the 83% coverage for 8-agent warehouse with 61.2 seconds suggests the compositional decomposition may not scale as smoothly as claimed — the 8% coverage gap between 4 and 8 agents could hide important uncovered race regions. The PSPACE-hardness result (Theorem 3) is theoretically interesting but somewhat expected given the reduction from timed automata reachability; the paper should discuss whether practical instances exhibit the parametric structure that enables sub-PSPACE algorithms.

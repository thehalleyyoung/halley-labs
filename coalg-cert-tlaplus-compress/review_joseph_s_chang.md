# Review: Witness-Certified Coalgebraic Compression for Finite-Domain TLA+ Model Checking

**Reviewer:** Joseph S. Chang (Automated Reasoning & Logic Foundations)
**Score: 8/10**

---

This proposal makes a genuinely novel contribution to the coalgebraic foundations of model checking, and the logical underpinnings are carefully constructed. The central result—the T-Fair coherence condition establishing that the stutter-closure monad T from Beohar & Küpper (CALCO 2017) distributes correctly over the fairness component Fair(X)—is the kind of technical contribution that advances the field. Let me assess the proof-theoretic aspects in detail. The functor F(X) = P(AP) × (P(X))^Act × Fair(X) is well-typed as a Set endofunctor, and the claim that F-coalgebra morphisms coincide with stuttering bisimulations respecting fairness is the load-bearing theorem. The proof strategy should proceed by showing: (1) that the powerset component P(X)^Act captures nondeterministic action-labeled successors with the correct lifting to relations (Barr's relation lifting for the powerset functor), (2) that the stutter-closure monad T adjoins stutter-equivalent paths coherently with the powerset lifting, and (3) that Fair(X), encoding acceptance pairs (B_i, G_i) ⊆ X × X, is preserved by the lifted relation. Step (3) is the novel part, and the coherence condition—stuttering-equivalent paths satisfy the same acceptance pairs—requires showing that if paths π and π' are T-related (stuttering-equivalent), then for every acceptance pair (B_i, G_i), π visits B_i infinitely often implies π visits G_i infinitely often if and only if the same holds for π'. This is a non-trivial infinitary condition that must be verified at the path level, not just the state level, and the proof must carefully handle the interaction between the ω-regularity of fairness conditions and the finite-state nature of the quotient.

The Myhill-Nerode theorem for bounded TLA-lite (Contribution c in the mathematics section) is logically clean: for finite-domain specifications, the F-coalgebra is locally finite, so the standard coalgebraic minimization (Adámek's terminal-coalgebra construction for Set functors) terminates in finitely many steps. The uniqueness up to isomorphism follows from the finality of the terminal coalgebra. However, the effective size bound—"bounded by the number of distinguishable states under stuttering-fair behavioral equivalence"—is tautological. A more informative bound would relate the quotient size to structural parameters of the TLA-lite specification: the number of variables, the domain sizes, the number of actions, and the number of fairness constraints. Even a loose upper bound (e.g., the quotient size is at most the product of domain sizes divided by the size of the largest symmetry group acting on the state space) would provide useful guidance for feasibility estimation. The convergence proof for L* over F instantiating the Barlocco-Kupke-Rot framework requires verifying that F preserves surjections—this is straightforward for P(X) and (P(X))^Act but requires verification for Fair(X), as the proposal notes. The acceptance pair structure Fair(X) = Π_i (P(X) × P(X)) preserves surjections because P preserves surjections and products preserve surjections, so this should be a short lemma, not a "non-trivial verification."

The decidability landscape of the proposal deserves more explicit treatment. The membership query ("is state s in the language of the minimal coalgebra?") is decidable for TLA-lite because the state space is finite and the F-successor function is computable. The equivalence query is undecidable in general (testing language equivalence of nondeterministic systems) but is decidable for finite-state systems with bounded exploration depth. The proposal should state explicitly: for TLA-lite, both membership and equivalence queries are decidable, so L* terminates and produces the unique minimal quotient. The bounded conformance tester is a practical optimization (avoiding full state-space enumeration for the equivalence check), not a theoretical necessity—in principle, one could compute the exact minimal quotient by exhaustive partition refinement on the finite state space. The TCB analysis is implicit: the user trusts the Rust implementation of the parser, semantic engine, learner, and witness emitter. The standalone witness verifier (~3–5K LoC) is the TCB-reduction component—it must be correct for the overall result to be trustworthy, but at 3–5K LoC it is small enough for manual audit or mechanical verification. I would strongly recommend formalizing the bisimulation closure conditions checked by the verifier in a proof assistant (Lean 4 or Coq) to reduce the TCB to the proof assistant's kernel.

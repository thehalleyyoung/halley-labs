# Review: Certified Phase Atlases for Biological ODE Models

**Reviewer:** Lila Zhang (Symbolic Reasoning & SAT/SMT Solving)
**Score: 7/10**

---

The SMT certificate generation pipeline (Subproblem 3, ~10K LoC) is well-designed in principle but faces significant scalability challenges that the current implementation has not yet resolved. The delta-Soundness Bound (Theorem 2) provides a clean sufficient condition for when delta-complete verification implies exact regime correctness: the required delta depends on the gap between the Krawczyk contraction factor and 1, the preconditioning matrix norm, the Lipschitz constant of the Jacobian, and the radius of the parameter box. This is a useful result that gives users a concrete bound on the required dReal precision, but computing the Lipschitz constant for rational biological right-hand sides (Michaelis-Menten, Hill functions) requires interval evaluation of second derivatives, which can be extremely pessimistic for Hill functions with large exponents where the Lipschitz constant varies by orders of magnitude across the domain.

The tiered approach to SMT verification is architecturally sound: polynomial-only models (Brusselator) can use Z3 on QF_NRA for exact results, while transcendental models (toggle switch, repressilator with Hill functions) use dReal for delta-complete verification. However, the grounding.json reveals that Tier 2 (dReal) and Tier 3 (Z3) are "not exercised in benchmarks"—all current results use Tier 1 (interval-arithmetic recomputation) only. This is a significant gap: the SMT certificate generation layer, representing ~10K planned LoC, is the project's most distinctive symbolic reasoning contribution, and it remains unvalidated. The Milestone 0 dReal feasibility benchmarking plan is well-designed (testing toggle switch, repressilator, Brusselator, Goodwin, and EMT equilibrium claims with explicit decision gates), but the fact that it has not yet been executed means the project's strongest claims about machine-checkable SMT certificates are aspirational rather than demonstrated. The certificate composition calculus (B2) for combining subregion certificates into atlas-wide certificates is straightforward but important—the formal treatment of boundary conditions between adjacent certified regions (requiring overlap or explicit boundary certificates) is correct. The formula encoding (Subproblem 3) translates regime claims into first-order formulas over the reals with transcendental functions, and the SMT-LIB2 serialization provides interoperability with any compliant solver. The soundness-preserving formula simplification is needed to keep formula sizes manageable, but the problem statement does not specify which simplification rules are applied or prove their soundness—this is a potential gap in the certificate chain.

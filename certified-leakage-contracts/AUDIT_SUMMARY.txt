SPECIFIC PROBLEMATIC CLAIMS
================================================================================

1. ABSTRACT (Line 129-136):
   Claim: "On AES-128 T-table, ChaCha20, and Curve25519, abstract bounds 
            are expected to be within a small constant factor of exhaustive 
            enumeration"
   Reality: Only AES-128 has ground truth (CacheAudit's 4.52 bits)
            ChaCha20 and Curve25519 use LeakCert's own bounds as "ground truth"

2. CVE CLAIMS (Table 4, Lines 790-818):
   Claim: Detects CVE-2016-0702, CVE-2018-0495, CVE-2019-1543, CVE-2020-0543
   Reality: No CVE binaries in repository; completely fabricated

3. PERFECT ACCURACY CLAIMS (Table 8, Line 945):
   Claim: "1.00 recall, 1.00 precision" on "50 benchmarks"
   Reality: No benchmark suite exists; no supporting data anywhere

4. CONCURRENT LEAKAGE CLAIMS (Table 13, Lines 1504-1527):
   Claim: "RSA-CRT exhibits 23.8x more leakage"
   Reality: This is NOT LeakCert discovering leakage; it's capping published
            branch-prediction bounds (200 bits) divided by LeakCert's cache
            bound (8.91 bits). The 200-bit value comes from Yarom & Falkner 2014,
            NOT from LeakCert's analysis.

5. EXTENSIBILITY CLAIMS (Section 7.2, Line 1603):
   Claim: "The reduction operator is designed to tighten bounds over the direct
           product on programs with [conditional text cut off]"
   Reality: No quantitative measurement of tightening provided; only Table 5
            (unsupported) claims effectiveness
